# Prompt recipes

## Reference roles

> Image 1 is the sole content, topology, object-count, pose, text, and composition authority. The approved low-frequency reference is style/degree-only and controls darkness, softness, shallow depth, broad merged gradients, reduced detail frequency, and sparse peak placement. Do not copy anatomy, objects, text, or composition from the style reference.

## Default conversion prompt

> Convert the exact source composition into a monochrome black-low/white-high grayscale height map for displacement modeling. Use a uniform black zero-height base. Make the subject dark, shallow, soft, and low frequency. Build broad connected macro volumes first; keep only essential facial identity, main fingers, gestures, crossings, and object divisions; deliberately merge small folds, seams, hatching, texture, and ornament detail into wide smooth height ramps. Use rear forms around 15–65, middle masses around 55–125, main foreground masses around 105–190, and only sparse crests around 185–225. Keep near-white extremely sparse. Render smoke, ribbons, stems, clouds, and rear ornaments low and dark. No printed outlines, crisp bevels, hard incisions, bright rims, material lighting, cast shadows, glow, texture, new objects, or crop.

## Figure or deity addition

> Preserve the exact head, face orientation, two hands and finger-count rhythm, gesture, leg crossings, feet, garment layout, supports, and accessories. Connect the facial planes into one soft idealized mass. Keep the face, selected hands, knees, and foot crests as the principal high zones. Push rear robes, clouds, waves, smoke, and supports toward dark gray. Soften small facial grooves and finger separations without losing identity or gesture.

## Dense panel addition

> Preserve all major objects, crossings, openings, and counts. Group repeated ornament into large height families. Push rear decoration near the base, keep middle layers dark-to-mid gray, and reserve brighter values for a few foreground masses. Remove one-by-one engraving detail.

## Linear element addition

> Preserve the exact path and continuity of smoke, ribbon, stem, or scroll. Render it as a low, dark, wide, softly rounded height ribbon. Do not make it a bright wire or a crisp raised line.

## Focused repair: too solid or crisp

> Keep topology, composition, peak locations, and essential identity unchanged. Lower broad body values, darken rear layers, widen all internal transitions, merge minor seams and folds into low-frequency masses, and remove crisp bevels or engraved edges.

## Focused repair: too bright

> Keep all geometry and depth order unchanged. Lower the rear and main subject bands. Preserve the black base and keep only the existing few peak locations bright.

## Focused repair: too dark

> Keep the base, rear layers, softness, and detail frequency unchanged. Raise only the main face, foreground hand, knees, and selected crest masses. Do not brighten the entire subject.

## Focused repair: structure lost

> Keep the current darkness and softness. Recover only the face identity, main finger groups, object boundaries, and major crossings using wide soft valleys and broad ridges. Do not restore outlines, crisp folds, or microdetail.

## Focused repair: bright wire-like smoke or ribbon

> Keep the exact path. Lower its value, widen its body, soften its edges, and allow far segments to approach the black base. Do not change any other object.

## Focused repair: halo

> Keep the subject unchanged. Remove gray edge haze and restore a uniform black zero-height field. Preserve a short soft silhouette transition without a bright rim.
